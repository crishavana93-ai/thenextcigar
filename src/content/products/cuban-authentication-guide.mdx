The counterfeit Cuban-cigar market is bigger than the legitimate one. Every aficionado will, at some point, be offered a "deal too good to refuse" in a hotel lobby in Havana, on a beach in Varadero, or via a sketchy WhatsApp seller. **This guide is the answer to the question you'll be asked: is it real?**

## What's inside

- **The 12-point authentication checklist** — printable card you can keep in your humidor
- **Box codes decoded** — every Habanos S.A. factory mark, region code, and date stamp explained with photos
- **Band and ring forensics** — paper grain, ink density, hologram positions, embossing depth (with high-res photo references for every major Habanos brand)
- **The eight most common scams** — and the tells that give them away
- **Buying online safely** — vendor red flags, shipping origin checks, and the four "always confirm" details
- **Buying in Havana** — La Casa del Habano vs. street, what's safe to buy in your hotel, what's never safe
- **Aging vs. fake** — telling a poorly stored real cigar from a counterfeit
- **The grey areas** — Cuban Cohibas vs. Dominican "Cohiba" (the IP dispute), and why both are legitimate in their own market
