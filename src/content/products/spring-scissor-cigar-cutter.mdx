Some cuts deserve a different tool. The scissor cut is the original cigar opening — a ritual cut that predates guillotines by a century. The blade comes in from both sides at once, taking a thinner sliver than a guillotine and leaving a wider draw without splitting the cap.

**Why scissors over a guillotine:**

- **Wider draw** — the V of the scissor's bite opens up airflow on torpedo and pyramid heads in a way a flat cut won't
- **Slower, more deliberate** — you can stop mid-cut and reposition. Guillotines are commit-and-go.
- **Built for figurados** — Salomones, perfectos, and shaped cigars cut cleanly without crushing
