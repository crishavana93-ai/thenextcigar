The gift-category piece. The lounge corner you've claimed at home deserves an ashtray that doesn't slide around when you set a Robusto down. This is that ashtray.

**The form:** 6-inch round, heavy lead-glass crystal, four deep cigar wells at 12, 3, 6, and 9 o'clock. Polished base with rubber pads to prevent surface scuffing.

**The weight:** 1.2kg empty. The whole point. A lightweight ashtray feels like something you'd find at a bad hotel — this one feels like a piece of furniture.

**What we're testing for:**

1. Weight ≥1kg (the real-crystal threshold)
2. Rim — finger around the entire edge, no nicks or chips allowed
3. **Wells fit a 50RG Robusto** — cigar should rest stable, not roll out when the ashtray is tipped slightly
4. Base stability — should glide without tipping when nudged
5. Optical clarity — held up to light, clear with no bubbles
6. **Tap test** — should ring like a wine glass (real crystal); dull thud means cheap soda glass

**Use case:** Single-cigar use or shared with one friend (4 wells = 4 cigars in rotation). The shared smoke is what cigar culture is built on, and a working ashtray for two people is genuinely scarce in the under-$100 market.

**Pricing reality:** Comparable crystal ashtrays at Davidoff or Mr Porter sell at $120–250. TNC pricing reflects direct sourcing.
