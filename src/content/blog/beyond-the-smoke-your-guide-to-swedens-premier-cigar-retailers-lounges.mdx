Sweden's cigar landscape is a nuanced blend of tradition and modern appreciation. While smoking regulations are strict, dedicated establishments have found ways to cultivate a thriving community for cigar lovers. Whether you're in search of a rare Cuban, a boutique New World blend, or simply a comfortable chair to light up, this guide will help you navigate the best of what Sweden has to offer.

**Important Note for Visitors:** Due to Sweden's strict tobacco legislation, indoor smoking areas are highly regulated. Many "lounges" might be private clubs, or dedicated smoking rooms within hotels or shops that comply with specific ventilation requirements. Always check their latest policies or book ahead. Opening hours, especially for smaller shops or lounges, can vary, so it's always wise to check their official websites or call ahead before visiting. Ownership details are often not publicly advertised for individual retail outlets.

## Stockholm: The Capital's Cigar Havens

**1. Brobergs Tobakshandel (Stockholm)**

- **Location:** Sturegallerian 39, 114 46 Stockholm (likely within the shopping gallery)
- **Known For:** A historic tobacconist with deep roots, Brobergs is often cited as a premium cigar retailer and Habanos Specialist. They are known for a wide selection of Cuban cigars, and often New World cigars and pipe tobacco.
- **Opening Hours:** As a shop within a gallery, expect typical mall hours (e.g., Mon-Fri: 10:00-19:00, Sat: 10:00-18:00, Sun: 11:00-17:00). **Always verify via their website or a call.**
- **Ownership:** Part of the Brobergs chain, which dates back to 1881. The larger entity might be part of a group, but the specific ownership of the local store is likely part of the Brobergs company structure.

**2. Cigarrklubben (The Cigar Club)**

- **Location:** Central Stockholm (exact address typically shared upon registration, as it's a private club)
- **Known For:** A private, members-only cigar lounge offering 24/7 access to its members. They do have limited public hours (Fridays & Saturdays 12:00-18:00) but require prior booking and registration. This is a true haven for serious aficionados to enjoy their own cigars in a controlled environment. They do not sell tobacco or alcohol, but allow members to bring their own (with a serving fee for drinks).
- **Opening Hours:** Members: 24/7 access. Public: Fridays & Saturdays 12:00-18:00 (by pre-booked appointment only).
- **Ownership:** A private membership club, indicating a club board or founders manage its operations.
- **Details:** Guests must register beforehand and await approval. Smoking room has strict rules (no food/drinks inside, only cigars).

**3. Stockholm Arlanda Airport Duty Free**

- **Location:** Stockholm Arlanda Airport (ARN)
- **Known For:** Duty-free shops often carry a selection of premium cigars, particularly for international travelers. While not a dedicated lounge, it's a significant retail point for those flying in or out.
- **Opening Hours:** Generally aligned with airport operating hours, which are very extensive.
- **Ownership:** Part of the larger airport retail operations (e.g., Avolta Group).

- **Opening Hours:** Typical mall hours (e.g., Mon-Fri: 10:00-19:00, Sat: 10:00-18:00, Sun: 11:00-17:00). **Verification advised.**

## Varberg: A Unique Cigar Destination

**1. Hotell Havanna - The Cigar Lounge**

- **Location:** Lindesbergsvägen 32, 432 52 Varberg
- **Known For:** This hotel boasts a uniquely Cuban-inspired atmosphere, featuring a renowned rum bar and a dedicated cigar lounge. It's designed to be a comfortable place for guests and visitors to enjoy cigars, often with a selection from their humidor.
- **Opening Hours:** As a hotel lounge, likely accessible during hotel operating hours, but specific lounge hours might apply. **Contact the hotel directly.**
